ExpressiveSinger: Synthesizing Expressive Singing Voice as An Instrument


Abstract:

In this study, we introduce ExpressiveSinger, the first end to end expressive singing voices synthesis model, which accurately reflect users’ musical expression by analyzing real-played MIDI sequences and lyrics. Unlike real-played MIDI sequences that contain velocity labels, MIDI sequences from SVS datasets do not have velocity information. We propose a novel method to automatically annotate the missing velocities in MIDI sequences, providing extra levers for singing generation. Moreover, we separately model expressive features and modify the vocoder to enhance controllability and quality of the synthetic singing voices. Finally, we adopt a soft-vc like approach for end-to-end training to effectively preserve more content features. Our experiments on the professional Mandarin singing corpus validate our data annotation method and demonstrate the effectiveness of ExpressiveSinger in terms of naturalness and a strong correlation between the synthetic singing voice and the MIDI input.
